# Origin Story

## The story

It starts in Varanasi, not in a startup. Before there was a job title, there was an office assistant, then a developer, then someone teaching IT faculty classes — all at once, all before the age of most people's first "real" job. That's not a humble-brag detail; it's the pattern that never left: learn something, immediately turn around and use it, then turn around again and teach it to someone else. Build. Learn. Share. Repeat. — the motto came decades later, but the loop was already running in 1997.

Five years at GITI/GSPL, inside a CDAC centre, building custom ERP software in VB and web applications in ASP.NET for a large government organization — not glamorous, but formative in a specific way: government-scale software has to actually work, for real people, for years, with no venture money cushioning the mistakes. That's a different discipline than building a demo.

Then the long middle: Dish TV, Binary Semantics, NIIT Technologies, HCL, Voith-Siemens — a decade-plus of enterprise systems, offshore development centers, and the unglamorous but real work of building teams from nothing (0 to 25, 0 to 50 people) and making them productive. This is where "systems builder" stopped being a metaphor. The systems weren't just software — they were teams, processes, offshore delivery centers, vendor relationships. Somewhere in Noida, growing a team from 15 to 25 people and watching the revenue they generated grow with them, the realization set in: building an organization *is* an engineering problem, just with people instead of components.

Digitas India and Competentia extended the range further — Nordic clients, Japanese automotive clients, Singaporean energy companies, global digital transformation work spanning EMEA, APAC, and the Americas. Different industries, different countries, same underlying question every time: what system needs to exist here, and what's the simplest one that actually works?

Then Norway. Cegal's integration modernization, Leasi's full CTO mandate, and — starting in 2025 — the shift that changes the shape of everything ahead: founding, rather than being hired to lead. Cognosic's AI-assisted SaaS platform for universities. ServiceBuddy's services marketplace, built AI-first from day one. Stealth (WIP), a vehicle for building and holding what comes next. The technology changed completely — Azure replaced BizTalk, RAG and LLMs replaced integration middleware as the interesting problem — but the underlying identity didn't move an inch. It was never about Azure. It's not about AI either. It's about building systems that create leverage, in whatever medium the current decade hands you.
