# Founder Story

## The story

For most of 27 years, the pattern was: join, inherit a mandate, execute it well, move on to a bigger mandate. Even the CTO titles — Cegal, Leasi — were still that pattern at its most senior level. Someone else owned the company; the job was to own the technology within it.

Cognosic broke the pattern without fully leaving it. CTO on paper, but in a lean startup environment there's no org chart deep enough to hide behind — leading technology, product, and architecture strategy for an AI-assisted SaaS platform meant doing the actual engineering (OpenAI integration, Flutter/Dart mobile, GCP and Neo4j infrastructure) alongside the strategic work, in a six-month window where the two were inseparable. It was the closest thing to founding without the equity and the exposure.

January 2026 is where it actually happened. Two things at once, deliberately: Stealth (WIP), a holding and venture vehicle for building and owning what comes next, and ServiceBuddy, as CPTO, leading product and technology for a services marketplace built AI-first from day one. Not a hired mandate this time — direct ownership of the outcome, the risk, and the upside.

Why now, after 27 years of being excellent at executing someone else's vision? Because the pattern recognition finally outpaces the learning curve. Multi-tenant SaaS architecture, enterprise integration at scale, team-building from zero, AI-assisted engineering — none of that had to be learned from scratch to found something in 2026. It had already been learned, repeatedly, in other people's companies. Founding is where all of it compounds instead of resetting with each new employer.
