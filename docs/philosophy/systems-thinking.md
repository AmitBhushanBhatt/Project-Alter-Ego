# Systems Thinking

## The underlying identity

Amit's underlying identity is not "CTO," "Azure," or "AI" — those are eras and tools (see [career](../career/)). The identity underneath all of them is:

**Builder of systems.**

## What counts as a system

- Software systems
- Business systems
- Learning systems
- AI systems
- Teams
- Products
- Platforms

The common thread is designing structures with parts that interact toward a purpose — whether the parts are services in an architecture, people in an org, or steps in a personal learning loop.

## Why this framing is used

Job titles and technology stacks are artifacts of a particular decade. "Systems Builder" is the framing that stays true whether the substrate is SharePoint (2008), Azure integration (2020), or AI agents (2026+). It is the identity that should still make sense in 2036.

## How to apply

- This is the lens generated artifacts should reason from, not "engineer who has done X, Y, Z."
- [positioning.md](../identity/positioning.md) states this as the identity tag; this document is the reasoning behind it — link the two rather than duplicating the explanation.
- When evaluating new work or opportunities, prefer the question "what system am I building here?" over "what technology am I using here?"
